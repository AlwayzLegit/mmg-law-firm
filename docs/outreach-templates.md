### 1b. Worked example — car-accident query

> *Query: "What's the first thing people get wrong after a car accident?"*

```
Quoting me as: Mihran M. Ghazaryan, personal injury attorney, MMG Law Firm
(Glendale, CA) — https://mmg-lawfirm.com

The most common mistake I see is giving a recorded statement to the other
driver's insurer before understanding their injuries. In California, you're
generally not obligated to give that statement, and adjusters are trained to
get you to minimize symptoms on the record. The smarter first steps: get
medical care even if you feel "fine" (soft-tissue and head injuries often
surface days later), photograph the scene and vehicles, and get the names of
any witnesses before they leave.
```

### 1c. Worked example — employment query

> *Query: "Can my employer fire me for any reason in an at-will state?"*

```
Quoting me as: Mihran M. Ghazaryan, employment attorney, MMG Law Firm
(Glendale, CA) — https://mmg-lawfirm.com

"At-will" is widely misunderstood. In California it means an employer can fire
you without cause — but not for an *illegal* reason. They can't fire you
because of a protected characteristic (race, sex, age, disability, pregnancy,
religion, national origin), in retaliation for reporting harassment or wage
violations, or for taking legally protected leave. If a termination lines up
suspiciously with one of those events, that timing is often the first thread
worth pulling.
```

### 1d. Topic bank (pre-write 1–2 lines each so you can answer fast)

Personal injury: recorded-statement traps · medical liens · how comparative

Employment: at-will limits · FEHA protected classes · meal/rest-break
penalties · final-paycheck timing rules · retaliation timing · how to document

accept a concrete headline with an angle their readers want.

CA. I read {{their site}} regularly — {{one honest specific: a recent post you
liked}}.

  {{One sentence on the angle and why their audience cares.}}

It'd be ~{{800–1,100}} words, plain-English, California-specific, with no
sales pitch — just genuinely useful guidance, plus a short author bio. I can
have a draft to you within a week.

Would that be a fit? Happy to adjust the topic to your calendar.

Mihran M. Ghazaryan

## 6. Review-request templates (lift Google/Avvo/Yelp ratings)

Reviews aren't backlinks, but review count + recency feed the same local
ranking that the citations support. Send only to clients whose matter has
concluded. **Never** offer anything of value in exchange for a review (bar +
platform rules).

### 6a. Google review request (SMS / email)

```
Hi {{first name}} — it was a privilege to work with you. If you have a moment,
a short review about your experience with our team would mean a lot and helps
others find us: {{Google review link}}. No pressure at all — thank you either
way. — Mihran, MMG Law Firm
```

### 6b. Avvo review request

```
Hi {{first name}} — thank you again for trusting MMG Law Firm. If you'd be
willing to share your experience, Avvo reviews are especially helpful for
people researching an attorney: {{Avvo profile link}}. Grateful for your time.
— Mihran
```

> **Compliance note:** keep review requests neutral — ask for "your honest
> experience," never a specific rating, and never tie a review to a discount,
> gift, or fee change.
